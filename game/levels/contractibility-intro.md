A type is **contractible** when it has a center to which everything is equal: `is-contr A` packages a center point and, for every other point, a path to it. Its two projections recover the center and that family of paths. This is the shape the Segal condition uses later, so reading an `is-contr` proof is the skill to take away.

The two projections are pre-tests. The closing puzzle, that any two points of a contractible type are equal, stays mandatory.

*By the end you will be able to:* extract the center of contraction, extract the contracting homotopy, and show that any two points of a contractible type are equal.
