A **Σ-type** `Σ (x : A), B x` is the type of dependent pairs: a point `x : A` together with a point of `B x`. Its projections `first` and `second` recover the two halves. This section forms pairs, projects them, and shows that a path between pairs gives a path between their first components.

These are standard, so the openers are pre-tests. The path-on-components puzzle stays mandatory. It is the move the later uniqueness-of-composites argument rests on.

*By the end you will be able to:* form a dependent pair, project out its components, and turn a path of pairs into a path of first components.
